Models of Comm

Transmission: unidirectional (sender to receiver), means, little to no context

Interactive: bidirectional (sender and receiver),
psycho-social relation, little context

Transactional: context is generative,
communicators instead of sender/receiver,
emphasis on norms and culture

Models + Forms of Comm

Transmission :: Mass

Interactive :: Intrapersonal, Organizational

Transactional :: Interpersonal, Intrapersonal

Here, I'm suggesting where each model leans. All forms of comm are inevitably present in the practice of every model.

Prompt 5

Miscommunication is arguably the most common
type of communication, partly because no
communication is somehow free of noise.
Take a minute to record some noises.

Prompt 5

Use 30-90 seconds of video or audio to record two
noises you frequently encounter during your
everyday communications. They should correspond with two different categories of noise found in the "Miscommunication" handout.

Then use no more than 350 words, including
terminology from the “Miscommunication” handout, to share 1) the two categories of noise you identified,
2) descriptions of the two noises, 3) how you
personally experience each noise, and 4) what each noise tells us about what people want or expect
from everyday communication.

Prompt 5

Consider these questions:

What's the desired signal here?
What interferes with it?

How is noise a form of direct or indirect feedback?

How do people (including you) respond to
that feedback?
