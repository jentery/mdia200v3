Secrets Agent by Marina Kittaka

That One Tip

Prompt 1: Revisit the top of Page 2 in the
"Content Industry" handout and consider not only
the pressure on freelance content creators to
push their brand but also the lack of supports
and protections in the industry.

That Other Tip

Prompt 2: Recall from the "Acts of Comm"
handout (middle of Page 2) that verbal and
nonverbal comm are always contextual, and
from the "Senses of Comm" handout (top of
Page 2) that alt text matters for inclusive communication.

The Next Tip

Prompt 3: Remember from the "Means" handout (Page 2) that infrastructure informs how we relate
both one-to-one and one-to-many and from
the "Levels" handout (Page 2) that the possibility
of alienation (like shouting into the abyss) can
shape the subtle differences between
interpersonal and mass comm.

Tip Fo(u)r You

Prompt 4: I ask you to share one context for
encoding and another for decoding. There's a list of pertinent contexts on Page 2 of the "Models"
handout, and Page 3 details Stuart Hall's dominant,
negotiated, and oppositional modes of decoding.
Also, don't underestimate the ways intrapersonal
and interpersonal comm (see "Forms," Page 1)
provide indirect feedback for mass comm,
including advertisements.

Last Tipppppppp

Prompt 5: The "Miscommunication" handout
refers to several types of noise that are difficult to record. Have fun with it. Don't hesitate to stage
or even fabricate a recording (like what psychological or relational noises sound like). Film, tv, and video
games do this all the time, and they often use
filters and sound effects in the process.
