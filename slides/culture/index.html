Cultural Studies Research

Not the effects of media
but the habits of media practice

Emphasis on shared values and beliefs (ideology)

The linear transmission of messages matters
less than everyday rituals.

Culture is an activity, not something
we possess ("cultured").

Cultural Studies Research

What are the habits of subcultures?
(the expressive rituals of subordinate groups)

What are the habits of perception?
(e.g., how we learn to look; the cis white male gaze)

What are the habits of performance?
(e.g., how others see us; impressions management)

Power is demonstrated publicly and
privately through habit.

Today's Case Study

Let's identify two active subcultures:
one known for "residual" media practices and
another for "emergent" media practices.

What's their style? What do they value?
What's their public impression?

How do they express their styles and values
through media? What's subcultural about
their habits and content?

"Residual": the persistence of old media today

"Emergent": seemingly new and distinct from
the norm (not the dominant mode)

The more specific the subculture, the better.
I'll take notes as the discussion unfolds.
